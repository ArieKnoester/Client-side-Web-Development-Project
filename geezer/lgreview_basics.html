The Legend of Grimrock 2

Basics:

Don't like the default group? Customize them!

You can either use the default party or create your own. I found the default party to work just fine, but the options are there if you love customization. You can choose any combination of 5 races and 8 classes. There are three levels of difficulty: easy, normal, and hard. If that’s enough for you there are additional difficulty options. Ironman mode will only allow saving the game at healing crystals. Old School mode will turn off auto-mapping, which means you’ll have to keep track of it yourself with paper and pencil. Finally Single Use Crystal Mode will only allow you to use the healing crystals once for each one you find. Normally you can use the crystals as many times as you want.

Plenty of areas to explore. Both above and below ground.

Movement:

Just like in the dungeon crawlers of old, the movement of your group of four characters is on a grid or tile system. The WASD keys will move the group forward, back, left and right; while the Q and E keys are used to turn the group left and right respectively. Your group will be positioned in a block with two characters up front and two in the rear. You control your group from a first person perspective, so turning to the left or right will turn the entire group to face said direction.

Younger gamers may find this mechanic to be a bit restrictive or archaic since there are no diagonals. Believe it or not it adds a level of complexity to the combat and getting used to it doesn’t take long. I highly recommend tapping the movement and turn keys instead of holding them down. There are plenty of traps and pitfalls. So you need to be deliberate in the movements, or you could find yourself in pain or aggravated if you aren’t careful.

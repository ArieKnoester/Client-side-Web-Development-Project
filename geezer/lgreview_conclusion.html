The Legend of Grimrock 2

Conclusion:

Nex is a big place! This is a map of just the surface level.

In the end, The Legend of Grimrock 2 was everything we were hoping for. Almost Human improved on the original in every way. The island is huge. The difficulty scales well. The music and sound design are excellent. The pacing between the combat and puzzle solving doesn’t get stale. The graphics are really quite good considering it’s an indie title developed by four people. On my system, frame rates were quite smooth on high settings. There were some noticeable drops here and there (mostly in heavily forested areas), but nothing so distracting that it ruined game immersion.

Final confrontation? The Island Master and his dragon mount.


Another great aspect of this game is that it comes with a level designer, so you can create your own content. You can even share your custom dungeons or play content created by others. This adds yet another level of replayability to the game. At $23.99 and over 20 hours of gameplay (not counting custom content), it was well worth the price.
